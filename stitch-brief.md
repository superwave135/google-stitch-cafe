# Fig & Filter — design brief

One document carrying everything a designer needs: the business, the brand, and the five pages with the job and content of each.

## The business

Fig & Filter is a neighbourhood café, open two years. It roasts its own coffee twice a week and bakes everything on site each morning. The website's audience is a stranger on a phone deciding whether to walk in, and an office manager on a laptop deciding whether to order catering. There is no online ordering and no booking system.

## Brand

| Element | Value |
|---|---|
| Name | Fig & Filter |
| Tagline | Roasted here. Baked here. Poured for you. |
| Primary colour | Deep fig purple #4A2C4D |
| Accent colour | Warm amber #D9A441 — accents only, never body text |
| Background | Warm off-white #FAF6F0 |
| Text colour | Near-black #221E1F |
| Headings | A serif with character |
| Body | A clean sans-serif |
| Voice | Warm, plainspoken, a little dry-humoured |

## Global rules

- Five pages, with the same navigation on every page: Home, Menu, Catering, About, Visit.
- Design mobile first; every layout must work in one narrow column.
- Every page has exactly one primary call to action; all other links are visually plain.
- Footer on every page: café name, opening hours, address (8 Chalk Lane), email, and an "Order catering" link.

## Page 1 — Home

**Job:** convince a stranger to walk in.
**Content:** the café name and tagline over a full-width photograph of espresso being poured; opening hours (Tuesday to Sunday, 7:30am – 5:00pm) and the address (8 Chalk Lane) near the top, visible without scrolling; three short reasons to visit (we roast our own; the kitchen bakes everything on site daily; stay as long as you like); one primary button labelled "Find us"; a secondary text link to the Menu.

## Page 2 — Menu

**Job:** let a visitor find what they would order in under a minute.
**Content:** four groups with prices — Coffee (espresso $3.50 to iced latte $6.00), Not Coffee (teas, chai, juices, $4.50–$6.00), All-Day Kitchen (toast $8.00 to big breakfast $22.00), Bakery (croissant $4.50 to banana bread $6.50); dietary markers (V, VG, GF); one photograph; single column on mobile, two columns maximum on desktop.

## Page 3 — Catering

**Job:** get an office manager to send a catering enquiry email.
**Content:** three packages, comparable at a glance — The Coffee Run $9 per person, The Morning Tea $16 per person, The Full Spread $28 per person; side by side on desktop, stacked on mobile; the ordering process (order by 2pm the day before, free delivery within 5km); one primary button labelled "Order catering" that opens an email.

## Page 4 — About

**Job:** make the café feel like people, not a business.
**Content:** the founding story (two years ago, in an old picture-framing shop, named after the fig tree in the courtyard); two founder profiles with portrait photographs — Mara (runs the kitchen, bakes from 5:40 every morning) and Dev (roasts twice a week on the machine behind the counter); a short note on the roasting schedule.

## Page 5 — Visit

**Job:** get someone through the door — address and hours, instantly.
**Content:** full address (8 Chalk Lane, corner of Weaver Street); opening hours as a table (Monday closed; Tuesday–Friday 7:30am–5:00pm; weekends 8:00am–5:00pm); how to find us; transit and parking notes; contact email and phone; a photograph of the shopfront. Nothing may push the address or hours below the first screen on mobile.
